## 1. Through-line of the week

The week established the *foundations* of both curriculum tracks and made the first deliberate cross-link between them. Two sessions built the Contemporaneous Records track from its root principle (contemporaneity) up to a usable grading rubric (ALCOA+ applied to a worked example). The third session opened the R&D Management track at its own foundation (portfolio governance). The connective insight: the same discipline that makes a *record* defensible — completeness including failures, honest capture of what was believed before the outcome — is the discipline that makes an *R&D portfolio* and, downstream, an R&D tax/innovation claim defensible. Records integrity and R&D management are the same habit viewed at two scales.

## 2. By track

### Track A — Contemporaneous Records (2 sessions)
- **2026-06-12 — Contemporaneity as master principle.** Of the nine ALCOA+ properties, contemporaneousness is the only one that cannot be retrofitted; this is why backdating is treated as fraud. Anchored in FRE 803(6) and the memory/hindsight/incentive degradation mechanism.
- **2026-06-13 — ALCOA+ as a grading rubric.** Battery-cycling dissected example: the polished from-memory summary fails Original/Complete and is the *weaker* record; recorded failures strengthen the evidentiary case.

### Track B — R&D Management (1 session)
- **2026-06-14 — R&D as a portfolio of bets.** Cooper's three macro-goals (value maximization, balance, strategic alignment). Ranked risk-adjusted NPV alone produces an individually-optimal but misshapen slate; a balanced slate deliberately funds a lower-scored project over a higher-scored one. Strategic buckets and the Stage-Gate-vs-portfolio distinction introduced.
