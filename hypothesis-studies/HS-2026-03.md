## Master hypothesis
Effective R&D management is governed at two complementary levels — **portfolio** (value maximization, balance, strategic alignment; Cooper/Edgett/Kleinschmidt) and **project** (stage-gate kill discipline) — and selection by any single criterion (NPV alone, or deliverable completeness) systematically underperforms.

## Progression of the inquiry
1. **RUN-007 (4 Jun)** — Finance-dominant prioritisation underperforms buckets + scoring. *Confirmed.*
2. **RUN-010 (7 Jun)** — Stage-Gate's value is its kill-rate; gates need stop authority. *Confirmed.*
3. **RUN-013 (10 Jun)** — Governance vs bureaucracy = reducing the killer uncertainty, not deliverable completeness. *Confirmed.*
4. **RUN-016 (14 Jun)** — Ranked risk-adjusted NPV misshapes the slate; balance + alignment beat single-metric ranking. *Confirmed.*

## Evaluation
Master hypothesis **supported** across both governance levels. The track is thin relative to the records track (4 sessions vs 10) and has not yet reached applied tooling.

## Stopping criteria / next
Mature the study with **technology roadmapping**, **R&D metrics / productivity** (e.g. Stevens), **managing scientists/engineers**, and **IP strategy** before a closing synthesis.
